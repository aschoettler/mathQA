Investigation

Say we're drawing our lines within the 1 by 1 square with one corner at (0,0)(0,0)(0,0) and the other at (1,1)(1,1)(1,1).

One thing to notice is that the strategy used to draw the lines is to start with a vertical one along the left side of the square, then shift the top endpoint down by some amount, and the bottom endpoint to the right by the same amount. In the first picture above, we shifted by 0.20.20.2 each time, and in the second picture, we shifted by 0.10.10.1 units between drawing lines. This leads to the following property:

If we call the endpoints of a line (0,u)(0,u)(0,u) and (v,0)(v,0)(v,0) then it must have intercepts that satisfy

u+v=1u + v = 1u+v=1

With this observation, there's nothing stopping us from drawing more lines with the same property. Using values of u,vu,vu,v that are outside the range [0,1][0,1][0,1] and extending them a little further beyond the points where they intersect the X and Y axes.

Analysis

The working theory is that if we draw infinitely many lines, the lines will perfectly trace out the exact curve.

Lets start by thinking about our collection of drawn lines as a family of lines which depend on a time parameter ttt. Suppose the line for ttt has the two points (t,0),(0,1−t)(t,0), (0,1-t)(t,0),(0,1−t). Then it turns out a point (x,y)(x,y)(x,y) is on this line if and only if (1−t)(x−t)+ty=0(1 - t) (x - t) + t y = 0(1−t)(x−t)+ty=0 Equivalently, t2−tx+ty−t+x=t2−(x−y+1)t+x=0t^2-t x+t y-t+x = t^2 - (x-y+1)t + x = 0t2−tx+ty−t+x=t2−(x−y+1)t+x=0 Now let F(t,x,y)=t2−(x−y+1)t+xF(t,x,y)= t^2 - (x-y+1)t + xF(t,x,y)=t2−(x−y+1)t+x

The easiest way to proceed is to notice that a point (x,y)(x,y)(x,y) lies on the curve if and only if there is exactly one value of ttt that generates a line that the point lies on. In this case, for such a special (x,y)(x,y)(x,y) value, there's 1 ttt such that F(t,x,y)=0F(t,x,y)=0F(t,x,y)=0. If we look at points (x,y)(x,y)(x,y) in the open white space, there are no lines through them meaning 000 t-values that work. And points "behind" the curve have multiple lines (so multiple t-values) corresponding to solutions to F(t,x,y)=0F(t,x,y)=0F(t,x,y)=0.
